Interesting Facts about Camel

  • Camels are large, long-necked ungulate mammal of arid country
  • It has long slender legs, broad cushioned feet, and either one or two humps on the back
  • Camels can survive for long periods without food or drink, chiefly by using up the fat reserves in their humps

Interesting Facts about Chameleon

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chaneleon
  • Most chaneleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Interesting Facts about Pigeon

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

Interesting Facts about TeddyBear

  • Teddy bear is a stuffed toy in the form of a bear.
  • They have larger eyes and foreheads and smaller noses, babylike features intended to enhance the toy's "cuteness".
  • They are a symbol of the care free moments of childhood and they remind us of being loved.

Interesting Facts about Panda

  • The panda is a bear native to South Central China
  • It is characterised by its bold black-and-white coat and rotund body.
  • It feeds almost entirely on bamboo and has become increasingly rare.

Interesting Facts about Cicada

  • The cicadas are a superfamily, the Cicadoidea, of insects in the order Hemiptera.
  • They are in the suborder Auchenorrhyncha, along with smaller jumping bugs such as leafhoppers and froghoppers.
  • The cicada will stay underground from 2 to 17 years depending on the species. Cicadas are active underground, tunneling, and feeding, and not sleeping or hibernating
